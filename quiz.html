1.What were the key reforms initiated by Jawaharlal Nehru for science and technology in India?


2.How does India's research output compare globally?


3.What is India's current ranking in the Global Innovation Index?


















































































































































































































































1. Key reforms by Jawaharlal Nehru for science and technology in India
~Nehru established several institutes of higher learning, including the Indian Institutes of Technology (IITs).
~He emphasized the importance of scientific temper and rational thinking in nation-building.
~Nehru also promoted state funding for scientific research and technological development.
2. India's research output comparison globally:
India's research output has been growing, particularly in fields
like engineering and computer science, but it still lags behind
major research-producing nations.
3. Global Innovation Index: India's ranking has been improving in recent years, placing it
within the top 50 countries, reflecting its growing innovation
capabilities.
